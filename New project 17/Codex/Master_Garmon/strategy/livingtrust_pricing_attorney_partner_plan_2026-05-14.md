## Attorney Review Positioning

Do not promise attorney review until a state-licensed attorney partner is contracted for the customer's state.

Use this language:

> LivingTrust prepares document-preparation files from your intake. It does not provide legal advice. Before signing or relying on any estate planning document, consult a licensed attorney in your state.

When attorney partners are added, sell the review as an optional or bundled upgrade:

- Attorney review add-on: target $300-$600 retail
- Attorney partner payout: target $150-$300 per review
- Customer-facing premium package: target $697-$1,297 depending on state and complexity

## Under-One-Hour Attorney Review Checklist

Goal: make a simple trust package review fit inside 30-60 minutes for a licensed attorney.

Attorney receives:
- Customer intake summary
- Grantor identity, residence, and state
- Family structure and relationship notes
- Trustee and successor trustee list
- Beneficiary list and distribution plan
- Asset inventory and funding schedule
- Risk flags from the intake assistant
- State execution checklist
- Draft trust document
- Draft pour-over will
- Draft certificate of trust
- Funding instructions
- Customer follow-up questions

Attorney checklist:
- Confirm customer state and whether attorney is licensed there
- Confirm no attorney-client relationship is implied unless separately accepted
- Check capacity, undue influence, identity, and signing concerns
- Review trustee powers and successor trustee order
- Review beneficiary names, shares, ages, and special-needs issues
- Review real estate and deed/funding implications
- Review retirement, life insurance, POD/TOD, and beneficiary-designation assets
- Review business interests, firearms, out-of-state property, and restricted assets
- Review tax, creditor, Medicaid, divorce, and blended-family flags
- Confirm signing, witness, and notary requirements for that state
- Return one of: approved for signing, changes requested, or attorney consultation required

## Compliance Boundary

Avoid:
- "Attorney-reviewed" unless an attorney actually reviewed that customer's file
- "Valid in every state"
- "Guaranteed to avoid probate"
- "No attorney needed"
- State-specific legal advice without counsel

Prefer:
- "Document preparation"
- "Review-ready"
- "State execution notes"
- "Consult a licensed attorney before signing"
- "Attorney partner review available where offered"
