## Mission

Make GAFC feel unavoidable in Sacramento streetwear by turning the brand into a repeatable viral drop machine: one hero product, one character, one neighborhood, one countdown, one proof loop, then repeat.

FertilityOS used a focused lead wedge, 25 execution lanes, daily KPI loops, and draft-first publishing. GAFC should use the same machine with streetwear-native content:

- Lead wedge: one limited local drop, not the whole catalog.
- Lead magnet: first access, address-by-SMS, raffle-only SKU, and product vote power.
- Conversion: SMS/waitlist signup first, purchase second.
- Proof: local comments, product votes, UGC, sellout recaps, line footage, and creator try-ons.
- Optimization: double down on the first hook, character, product, venue, or neighborhood that hits 2x baseline.

## KPI Rules

Baseline metrics must be set during the first 72 hours.

Double down when:
- Hook produces 2x comments, saves, shares, or link clicks.
- Character produces 2x comments or product votes.
- Creator produces 2x click-through or signup rate.
- Neighborhood produces 2x comment volume or venue tags.
- Product gets 30% or more of all votes.

Kill or rewrite when:
- Completion rate is low.
- Comments are confused about what is being sold.
- Post attracts firearm/violence interpretation.
- Product vote does not produce signups.
- CTA is buried or unclear.
