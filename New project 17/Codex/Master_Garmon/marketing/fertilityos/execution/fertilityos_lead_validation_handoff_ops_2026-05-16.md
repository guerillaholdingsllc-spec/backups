# FertilityOS Lead Validation And Handoff Operations Packet

Created: 2026-05-16
Owner lane: FertilityOS marketing operations
Applies to: FertilityFund, IVFCompare, MaleGenesis
Primary goal: Validate, qualify, document consent, and route leads to the correct internal or buyer handoff path without overstating medical, financing, clinic, or compliance claims.

## Operating Position

FertilityOS is an educational decision-support and lead-routing property. Treat every user record as sensitive, even when it is not confirmed PHI. Do not sell, transfer, or retarget reproductive health, fertility, insurance, or financing data unless the user consent record, partner contract, privacy notice, and channel rules have been reviewed for that exact use.

This packet is an operating guide, not legal advice. Use it to reduce avoidable risk and to prepare clean review materials for counsel, compliance, and buyer onboarding.

## Lead Status Definitions

- `new`: Raw form submission or quiz/calculator completion received.
- `needs_review`: Missing consent, unclear intent, sensitive free-text concern, duplicate, underage signal, or unsupported geography.
- `qualified`: Required fields, consent evidence, and routing criteria are present.
- `disqualified`: Spam, invalid contact, no consent, outside service area, underage signal, or explicitly requests no contact/sale.
- `nurture_only`: Useful educational contact but not appropriate for buyer handoff.
- `handoff_ready`: Qualified, tiered, deduped, and matched to a buyer or internal sales queue.
- `handed_off`: Sent to buyer/internal owner with timestamp and handoff channel recorded.
- `closed_loop`: Buyer/internal owner acknowledged receipt and disposition was logged.

## Minimum Qualification Rules

A lead may not be marked `handoff_ready` unless all required checks pass:

- Contact validity: Email or phone is present and plausibly formatted.
- Consent status: Consent language, timestamp, source page, and intended contact channel are captured.
- Intent signal: User selected or typed a fertility financing, clinic comparison, male fertility, benefits, or care-navigation need.
- Geography: State, metro, or ZIP is available when the buyer offer depends on location.
- Age gate: No known minor. If the record suggests the user is under 18, mark `disqualified` and do not hand off.
- Duplicate check: No same email/phone already handed off in the last 30 days unless the buyer explicitly accepts updates.
- Sensitive text review: Free-text content does not include urgent medical distress, crisis language, or information that should remain internal-only.
- Suppression check: User has not opted out, revoked consent, or requested deletion/no sale.

## Disqualification And Hold Rules

Put the lead in `needs_review` or `disqualified` when any of these appear:

- No documented consent for the intended outreach or transfer.
- User asks for medical diagnosis, treatment recommendation, pregnancy prediction, medication advice, or emergency help.
- User indicates immediate medical distress or self-harm risk; route to a human review path and do not sell.
- Lead is generated by a bot, competitor, scraper, or obvious fake identity.
- Lead asks only for general content and did not request contact.
- State, country, or buyer category has not been approved for the campaign.
- User revokes consent through any channel.

## Consent Requirements

Capture a consent record before outreach or partner handoff. Store enough detail to reconstruct what the user saw and agreed to.

Required consent fields:

- `consent_status`: `yes`, `no`, `revoked`, or `unknown`.
- `consent_scope`: Example values: `email_followup`, `sms_followup`, `phone_followup`, `partner_handoff`, `marketing_nurture`.
- `consent_language_version`: Internal version ID for the exact disclosure shown.
- `consent_timestamp`: ISO date/time where available.
- `source_url`: Page or campaign URL where consent was collected.
- `lead_source`: Campaign, organic page, paid ad, referral, or partner source.
- `opt_out_status`: `active`, `opted_out`, `deletion_requested`, `do_not_sell_or_share_requested`.

Channel-specific guardrails:

- Email: Use clear sender identity, purpose, unsubscribe mechanism, and campaign/source traceability.
- SMS/phone: Treat marketing texts and calls as requiring prior express written consent unless reviewed otherwise. Include the seller/brand, contact channel, and opt-out path in the consent record.
- Partner transfer: Do not hand off unless the user-facing notice supports the specific category of recipient and purpose.
- Retargeting/ads: Do not upload or share fertility, reproductive health, benefits, insurance, quiz, or calculator audiences for ad targeting without a reviewed privacy basis.

## Buyer And Handoff Tiers

Tier each valid lead by intent, urgency, data completeness, geography, and partner fit. Tiers are for routing and negotiation only; they are not outcome predictions.

### Tier A: High-Intent Handoff

Typical signals:

- Completed IVF cost calculator, financing pathway, clinic comparison, or employer benefits lookup.
- Provided contact details, geography, timeframe, and explicit partner/contact consent.
- Indicates active treatment planning within 0-90 days.

Best-fit buyers:

- Fertility lenders or financing marketplaces.
- Clinic networks with approved geography.
- Fertility benefit navigation vendors.
- Employer-benefits or insurance navigation partners.

Operating action:

- Human review within 1 business day.
- Route to exclusive or priority buyer only when consent and buyer contract support exclusivity.
- Require buyer receipt acknowledgement and disposition update.

### Tier B: Qualified Nurture Or Multi-Buyer Match

Typical signals:

- Strong topic intent but missing timeframe, geography, or one partner-fit field.
- User requested educational follow-up or comparison help.
- Consent supports follow-up but partner transfer may need confirmation.

Best-fit buyers:

- Non-exclusive financing, clinic, benefits, or navigation partners.
- Internal nurture sequence.

Operating action:

- Send education-first follow-up.
- Request missing fields before external handoff.
- Do not position as an exclusive lead until missing fields are resolved.

### Tier C: Research Or Content Lead

Typical signals:

- Download, newsletter, calculator abandon, low-intent quiz completion, or broad question.
- Consent supports owned follow-up only.

Best-fit buyers:

- Internal content nurture.
- Retain for aggregate campaign learning without sensitive audience exports.

Operating action:

- Do not sell.
- Use educational content and preference capture.
- Re-score only after a fresh action.

### Hold: Compliance Or Human Review

Typical signals:

- Consent gap, sensitive free text, medical distress, opt-out conflict, duplicate conflict, unsupported geography, or unclear user identity.

Operating action:

- Keep internal.
- Resolve the hold reason or suppress the record.
- Do not hand off while the hold is open.

## Pricing Bands For Planning

These are planning bands for buyer conversations, not guaranteed revenue, fair market value, or legal guidance. Final pricing should be validated against buyer demand, exclusivity, consent scope, geography, delivery speed, return policy, and compliance review.

- Tier A exclusive lead: `$125-$350` per accepted lead.
- Tier A non-exclusive lead: `$45-$150` per accepted lead.
- Tier B qualified lead: `$20-$75` per accepted lead.
- Tier C owned nurture lead: no external sale; value through internal conversion only.
- Appointment-set or warm-transfer lead: negotiate separately, commonly higher than form leads when consent and call recording rules are satisfied.
- Revenue share or referral model: use only after legal/compliance review of licensing, healthcare marketing, lender, broker, and state-specific rules.

Acceptance terms to define before launch:

- What counts as an accepted lead.
- Duplicate window.
- Bad-lead return reasons.
- Maximum return period.
- Required fields.
- Delivery SLA.
- Whether the lead is exclusive, semi-exclusive, or non-exclusive.
- Whether buyer may resell, retarget, or enrich the record.

## Handoff Workflow

1. Ingest the lead from form, calculator, quiz, or manual import.
2. Normalize fields into the tracking format.
3. Run validation checks: contact, consent, age, geography, duplicate, opt-out, and sensitive text review.
4. Assign lead status and buyer tier.
5. Match to approved buyer category.
6. Confirm allowed handoff scope against consent and partner contract.
7. Send handoff through the approved channel only.
8. Log delivery timestamp, recipient, channel, and payload version.
9. Request buyer acknowledgement and final disposition.
10. Close loop or place in remediation queue.

## Daily Operating Checklist

- Review all `new` leads from the prior day.
- Check opt-outs, consent revocations, and deletion/no-sale requests before any handoff.
- Deduplicate by email, phone, and obvious identity match.
- Review free-text fields for sensitive or urgent content.
- Assign tier, buyer category, and hold reason where needed.
- Send only `handoff_ready` leads to approved buyers.
- Log every handoff with timestamp, buyer, channel, and payload version.
- Confirm buyer receipt for prior-day handoffs.
- Update return/disposition fields.
- Record campaign totals: new leads, qualified leads, handoff-ready leads, handed-off leads, returns, opt-outs, and compliance holds.
- Sample at least 5 records or 10% of daily leads, whichever is greater, for consent evidence.
- Escalate any breach, unauthorized sharing, missing consent, or user complaint immediately.

## CSV-Friendly Tracking Format

Use one row per lead event or handoff. Keep values plain, comma-safe, and consistent. Avoid commas inside free-text fields; use semicolons if needed.

Recommended columns:

```csv
lead_id,created_at,updated_at,brand,source_url,campaign_id,asset_id,lead_source,utm_source,utm_medium,utm_campaign,contact_email,contact_phone,state,zip,intent_category,intent_detail,timeframe,consent_status,consent_scope,consent_language_version,consent_timestamp,opt_out_status,validation_status,validation_notes,buyer_tier,buyer_category,buyer_name,handoff_status,handoff_channel,handoff_timestamp,payload_version,buyer_ack_status,buyer_disposition,price_band,return_reason,compliance_hold_reason,next_action,next_action_owner,next_action_due
```

Field notes:

- `lead_id`: Internal ID; do not use email or phone as the ID.
- `brand`: `FertilityFund`, `IVFCompare`, or `MaleGenesis`.
- `intent_category`: `financing`, `clinic_compare`, `benefits`, `male_fertility`, `care_navigation`, `general_research`.
- `validation_status`: Use the lead status definitions in this packet.
- `buyer_tier`: `A`, `B`, `C`, or `Hold`.
- `price_band`: Store the negotiated or planning band, not a promised amount.
- `compliance_hold_reason`: Use consistent labels such as `missing_consent`, `opt_out`, `sensitive_text`, `minor_signal`, `unsupported_geo`, `duplicate`, `medical_request`.

## Practical Compliance Notes

- Keep marketing claims educational and evidence-aware. Avoid saying FertilityOS can diagnose, recommend treatment, guarantee financing approval, rank clinics, predict pregnancy, or confirm insurance coverage.
- Treat fertility, reproductive health, insurance, and financing data as sensitive even if a strict legal classification is unresolved.
- HIPAA may apply when working with covered entities or business associates; HHS notes HIPAA covered entities and business associates have limits on PHI use/disclosure, and marketing use of PHI often requires written authorization.
- FTC health privacy expectations may apply to health apps and sites outside HIPAA. The FTC Health Breach Notification Rule can require notice for breaches of unsecured identifiable health information by covered health technology vendors.
- TCPA and related FCC rules can apply to marketing calls and texts. Do not rely on implied consent for automated marketing outreach.
- California and other state privacy regimes may create opt-out, sensitive-data, deletion, access, and disclosure duties. Respect `do_not_sell_or_share` and sensitive-data limitation requests where applicable.
- Do not share raw lead data through personal email, spreadsheets with public links, unapproved chat tools, or ad audience upload tools.
- Keep a source-of-truth consent record. If the consent evidence cannot be reconstructed, do not hand off.
- Have buyers agree not to resell, misuse, retarget, or enrich lead data beyond the approved purpose unless separately reviewed and disclosed.
- Maintain a complaint and deletion workflow before scaling paid distribution.
